Search for Factors to Consider When Selecting Reading Materials
🔵 5 GROUPS of 4 — internet and print; take notes

In your group of 4, use an internet-enabled device and print materials to search for factors one should consider when choosing a text to read. Select your group tab below, tick each factor found, then save your group's notes.

Group Task: Search ("factors to consider when selecting reading materials") online or in your course book. Tick each factor your group finds and agrees belongs on the list. Save your findings, then write notes in your exercise books.
2
Identify Print & Non-Print Texts to Read
🔵 5 GROUPS — apply the factors from Step 1

Using the selection factors your group identified in Step 1, identify texts — print and non-print — that would be interesting and appropriate to read on the theme of Human Rights. Tap each text type to learn about it. Then select your group tab and record your group's choices.

Discuss Reasons for Reading
🔵 5 GROUPS — discuss; why reading for enjoyment matters

In groups, discuss various reasons people read and why reading for enjoyment is important. Tap the reasons your group agrees on, then select your group and answer the three discussion questions. Write answers in your exercise books.

💡
Reasons for Reading — tap all your group agrees on
🧠
Reading for Information
To learn facts, understand events, and find answers — e.g. about human rights in a newspaper.
😊
Reading for Enjoyment
To relax and have fun. Pleasure reading builds a lifelong love of learning and improves language naturally.
🔤
Reading to Improve Language
Wide reading expands vocabulary, improves spelling, and builds confidence in speaking and writing.
🌍
Reading to Understand Others
Stories build empathy and help us understand experiences very different from our own.
⚖️
Reading to Know Your Rights
Reading about human rights empowers us to understand, claim, and defend our rights and others'.
💼
Reading to Think Critically
Reading helps us form and defend opinions and think carefully about complex issues.

Go Through Sample Journal & Create Your Own
👤 20 INDIVIDUALS — each learner creates their own entry

Study the sample reading journal entry carefully to understand the format. Then select your name and fill in your own personal journal entry for a text you have read or a text your group chose in Step 2.

👁
Sample Reading Journal Entry — study this first
📓 Sample Entry
Date
Thursday, 8 January 2026
Title
The Rights of the Child — UNICEF
Type
Online Article (Non-print)
About
Explained the 10 basic rights every child is entitled to under the UN Convention.
Interesting
Over 300 million children still lack clean water — a basic right in the convention.
New words
Convention, Ratified, Entitled
Rights link
Directly about children's rights — the right to education, safety, and clean water.
Rating
★★★★☆
Recommend?
Yes — every learner should know their rights.
Notice: A good journal is personal and honest. Write in YOUR own words — your feelings and reactions. There are no wrong answers. This is your reading story.
