1️⃣
What is a Telephone Message?

A telephone message is a written record of information from a caller when the receiver is not available to speak. The person who answers writes it down accurately and passes it on.

When is it needed?
• A parent calls the headteacher who is in a meeting.
• You call a classmate but only their parent answers.
• An officer is on rounds when the community calls.
In your exercise book:
1. Define "telephone message" in your own words.
2. Give TWO situations in your community where a message would be needed.

3️⃣
Polite Language for Taking a Message
Saying person is unavailable:
"I am afraid Mary is not available. May I take a message, please?"
Asking caller's name:
"Could you kindly tell me your name, please?"
Asking for contact number:
"Could I have your telephone number, please?"
Confirming and closing:
"I will make sure she receives your message. Thank you for calling."

Role Play — Leave & Take Messages
👥 10 PAIRS — take turns; compare your written messages

In pairs, take turns: one is the caller leaving a message, the other is the receiver writing it down. Then swap roles. After both turns, compare your written messages — are they the same? Record your voices to listen back.
